Image Mosaicing

Project 4 — Homography, Warping, and Blending

A.1 Shoot and Digitize Pictures

I captured two sets of photographs by rotating my camera around a fixed center of projection. Each pair of images has a 40–60% overlap for reliable feature matching.

Example pair of images with projective transformation.

A.2 Recover Homographies

To compute the homography H between two views, I implemented the Direct Linear Transformation (DLT) algorithm. The system Ah = b was solved using least-squares with more than four correspondences for robustness.

Visualized correspondences between two images.

Example of recovered homography matrix: 

H =
| 1.002  -0.013   120.43 |
| 0.025   0.995   -20.17 |
| 0.0002  0.0001   1.000 |

A.3 Warp the Images

I implemented inverse warping with two interpolation methods:

  • Nearest Neighbor: Fast but blocky results.
  • Bilinear: Smoother and visually pleasing, but slower.

A.4 Blend Images into a Mosaic

I warped each image into the reference frame and blended them using alpha-weighted averaging. To avoid hard seams, I used a linear falloff mask that smoothly transitions near overlapping regions.
